What is the Difference Between Contested and Uncontested Divorce in Texas?

When it comes to family law matters specific to divorces in Texas, understanding the difference between an uncontested and contested divorce is essential. It is important to note that regardless of the approach, you need to be prepared that the matter will be a financial and emotional divorce.

An uncontested divorce is one where both parties agree on all aspects of the marriage dissolution, including division of property, child support and custody, spousal support, and any other relevant issues. On the other hand, a contested divorce is one in which at least one party disagrees with the proposed terms or conditions of the settlement.
