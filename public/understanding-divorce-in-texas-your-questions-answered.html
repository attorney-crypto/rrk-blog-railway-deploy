Understanding Divorce in Texas: Your Questions Answered

📅 January 25, 2026
✍️ Law Offices of RRK, LLC

Divorce can be a challenging and emotional process. At RRK Law, we strive to provide clear and concise information to help you navigate this difficult time. Below, we've compiled answers to some of the most frequently asked questions about divorce in Texas.

1. How much does a divorce lawyer cost in Texas? Divorce lawyers in Texas typically charge between $200 to $500 per hour, depending on their experience and the complexity of the case.

2. How much does it cost to file for divorce in Austin, Texas? The cost to file for divorce in Austin, Texas, is generally between $250 and $350, depending on the county and specific court fees.

3. How long does divorce take in Austin? Divorces in Austin typically take between 60 days to 6 months, but more complex cases can take longer.

4. What is the cheapest way to get a divorce in Texas? The cheapest way to get a divorce in Texas is through an uncontested divorce where both parties agree on all terms.

5. What is a wife entitled to in a divorce in Texas? In a divorce in Texas, a wife is entitled to an equitable share of marital property, which may include assets and debts acquired during the marriage.

6. How much does it cost to get a divorce if both parties agree in Texas? If both parties agree, the cost of a divorce in Texas can be as low as $300 to $500, covering filing fees and minimal legal assistance.

7. Does it matter who files for divorce first in Texas? Filing for divorce first in Texas does not provide any legal advantage, but it may give the filer control over certain procedural aspects.

8. How long do you have to be separated in Texas before divorce? In Texas, there is no mandatory separation period before filing for divorce.

9. Can a divorce be final without both signatures in Texas? Yes, a divorce can be finalized without both signatures if one spouse does not respond or contest the divorce.

10. Can a spouse refuse divorce in Texas? A spouse cannot refuse a divorce in Texas, but they can delay the process by contesting it.

11. What if one party doesn't want a divorce? If one party doesn't want a divorce, the other party can still file, and the court can grant the divorce based on irreconcilable differences.

12. Can I get a divorce without going to court in Texas? Yes, if both parties agree on all terms, you can finalize a divorce without going to court through an uncontested divorce process.

13. What is the 10-year rule in divorce in Texas? The 10-year rule in Texas may affect spousal maintenance, making it more likely to be awarded if the marriage lasted 10 years or more.

14. How to get a divorce with no money in Texas? You may qualify for a waiver of court fees if you can demonstrate financial hardship, and legal aid services may assist with your case.

15. How does a no-contest divorce work in Texas? A no-contest divorce in Texas is when both parties agree on all issues, allowing for a quicker and less expensive process.
