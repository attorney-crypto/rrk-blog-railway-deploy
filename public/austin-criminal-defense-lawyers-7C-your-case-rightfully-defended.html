Frequently Asked Questions

Is assault a felony and misdemeanor in Texas?

Yes, assault in Texas is both a misdemeanor and a felony, specifically a second-degree felony, when it is committed with a deadly weapon. It all depends on the types of assault you are charged with...

What is considered assault in Texas?

In Texas, an assault charge is brought up when someone intentionally or recklessly harms another person either physically, threatens harm verbally o,r uses offensive physical contact. If convicted of this crime, there are potential penalties such as prison time and hefty fines which can be severe depending on the situation. It’s important to understand your state laws so that you know what consequences come with committing an act of assault.

Is aggravated assault a felony in Texas?

In Texas, Aggravated Assault is considered a felony and the state takes this offense very seriously. Penalties can be harsh. Most cases result in 2-20 years imprisonment with fines of up to $10,000. It’s important to understand these consequences should someone ever be charged with such an offense, so one may take necessary precautions accordingly.

The legal repercussions associated with this type of crime must not be overlooked - hence why it’s extremely vital for individuals confronted by accusations like Aggravated Assault to know what steps they need to take to protect themselves properly.

What is simple assault in TX?

Simple assault, which is classified as a misdemeanor in Texas, can result in severe consequences: incarceration of up to one year and fines amounting to no more than $4,000. It involves deliberately or carelessly attempting or causing physical harm.

How much does a DWI lawyer cost in Texas?

When searching for a DWI attorney, take caution - the fees can range from as low as $3,000 to as high as $20,000 depending on how serious your case is. It’s important that you don’t just assume an attorney with lower costs will be able to handle it adequately. Make sure they have enough experience and expertise in dealing with similar cases first before making a decision.
