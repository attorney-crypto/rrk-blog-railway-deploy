Should I Contact the Police for a Minor Car Accident?

In the event that someone is hurt, or damage to one or more vehicles exceeds $1000, it's essential to report the crash directly to the police. The officer in charge must then fill out a Peace Officer’s Crash Report (CR-3 form).

There will be no need for you to complete your own crash report if an officer has already done so. Even though the collision may seem minor, it is always best practice to alert authorities and allow them to document who was at fault as well as assess any damages; this information can prove invaluable when dealing with insurance companies.

Failure to report a serious accident involving injury or property damage exceeding $1,000 is punishable with time in jail or a fine of up to $5,000, or both.
