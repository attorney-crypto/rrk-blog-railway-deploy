Q: What are 'Super Fines' in the context of DWI charges in Austin, and how can they affect my case?

A: In Texas, 'Super Fines' refer to additional financial penalties imposed on top of standard fines for DWI convictions. Here's a quick breakdown:

- Super Fines for DWI: These are extra monetary penalties if you're convicted of a DWI.

- Amount: You could pay up to an additional $6,000 on top of other fines.

- When They Apply:

First DWI: An additional $3,000.

Second DWI (within 36 months): An additional $4,500.

DWI with BAC of 0.15% or more: An additional $6,000.

Nature of the Penalty: These are criminal penalties attached to criminal DWI charges.

Avoiding Them: The most effective way to avoid these super fines is not to drive under the influence. However, if charged, your defense strategy—such as negotiating for a lesser charge—could influence whether you face these fines. The outcome heavily depends on the specifics of your case and the prosecutor's approach.

These super fines are designed to be severe enough to deter drunk driving. The best strategy to avoid these penalties is to stay sober when driving.
