What if Officers Responded and There Was a Fatality?

There is no doubt that motorcycle accidents can be fatal, and the consequences of such a crash can be life-altering. In Austin, Texas, and throughout the United States, these types of accidents are sadly very common and often result in fatalities of the driver or other vehicles involved.

When a motorcycle collision occurs and results in a fatality, there is understandable quite an extensive investigation undertaken by police and other legal authorities. This type of police investigation will typically involve interviews with witnesses and any other people who may have had knowledge of the incident.

The purpose of this is to determine what led to the crash and if any parties were negligent or reckless in their actions. Additionally, driving records for any drivers involved in the incident will usually be reviewed to see if they had prior histories of any traffic violations or other issues that may have caused or contributed to the fatal crash.

If it is determined that one particular party was at fault for causing the fatal motorcycle wreck, they could potentially face criminal charges due to their reckless behavior. These would depend on individual state laws but could include vehicular manslaughter, Driving While Intoxicated (DWI), operating while intoxicated, or other related offenses depending on what happened during the ordeal.

Moreover, if intoxication is suspected and investigated, the consequences of such a crash can be even more serious. There could be one, or two counts, maybe even multiple counts after the investigation is completed.

When a wreck involving a motorcycle happens, specialized crews are brought in to investigate the scene. These crews look for evidence of intoxication, whether it be from alcohol or drugs, and any other factors that may have caused the wreck. If it is determined that the driver of the motorcycle was drinking or under the influence at the time of the crash, then they will likely be charged with intoxication and manslaughter.

Intoxication manslaughter carries severe penalties, including prison sentences and high fines, so it should always be taken seriously. It’s important for drivers to understand their legal rights and obligations when operating a motorcycle on a public road, and to always practice safe driving habits to avoid being involved in an accident—especially one involving intoxication.

Those found guilty could also face civil suits brought against them by those affected by their negligence, as well as hefty fines imposed by legal authorities.

In most cases where a vehicle has crashed into a rider, and they are killed due to another party’s negligence, family members of the deceased may decide to take legal action against those responsible for wrongful death damages incurred from medical bills, funeral costs, lost wages from work missed due to injury or death, loss of companionship and emotional distress associated with the incident.

In order to pursue such a case successfully, it’s important for family members to seek legal advice from an experienced attorney with knowledge regarding personal injury claims resulting from fatal motorcycle accidents in Austin and throughout Texas.

It’s important for anyone involved in a motorcycle crash involving fatalities to be aware of their potential legal rights after such an event occurs so that judgment can be made as good as possible according to applicable state laws.
