What’s the difference between legal custody and physical Custody in child custody cases?

Parents typically divide both legal and physical custody of their child. But what do these two types of custody really mean? Legal custody is the right to make decisions that affect the upbringing and welfare of a child, while physical custody involves day-to-day responsibility for the child's parent in providing the actual caretaking required.

What is joint custody?

Generally, parents are granted joint custody, granting them both equal rights to the medical and educational records of their own child. It also requires that they collaborate on decisions involving their children together.

Unlike physical custody, joint legal custody has nothing to do with the child's place of residence. But by frequently awarding joint legal custody, a parent who is granted visitation rights or secondary physical custody will not be excluded from making major decisions involving their children.

Will the court order that my child lives with me or the other parent based on their decision?

In Texas, kids lack the autonomy to decide their primary residence until they turn twelve years old. At this time, a court may factor in the child's wishes when making its final decision on what parent they should reside with; though it is not necessarily binding.

And even now, the courts do not require that the child fill out an affidavit to that effect. Rather, they usually only the child's desire into consideration if they need extra evidence to make a decision, typically in Travis and Williamson County - in our experience.

Usually, this can happen when the evidence indicates both parents are good parents, the attorneys presented compelling evidence on both sides, and it's basically a stalemate.

Will a judge grant 50/50 custody in Texas?

Despite what you might have heard, it is possible to achieve 50/50 custody in Texas. In fact, the law explicitly states that gender discrimination cannot be a factor when making any child-custody decisions. Fathers don't need to fear they will get an unfair hearing if they seek equal parenting time with their children.

The other parent is saying I am unfit, what does that mean?

If a parent is deemed unfit to contact their child, they will be stripped of any legal right to possess or visit them. This includes situations in which there has been an occurrence of family violence, emotional abuse, or other potentially hazardous risks - such as within the household itself.

What would cause a mother to lose custody in Texas?

In Texas, there are certain occasions where parents may be stripped of their custodial rights over their children. Such situations can include but are not limited to child abuse, negligent parenting, or any kind of long-term mental health problems and/or drug addiction on the part of the parent(s). However, if this unfortunate circumstance happens, a family law attorney/visitation lawyer can work closely with the court to obtain visitation rights during this time.

Can a mother legally keep her child away from the father in Texas?

The law is clear that parents must parent their child custody matters together, in accordance with what will bring the most benefit for them. How much is minimum wage child support in Texas?

Texas child support laws provide the following Guideline calculations: one child= 20% of Net Monthly Income; two children = 25% of Net Monthly Income; three children = 30% of Net Monthly Income; four children = 35% of Net Monthly Income; five children = 40% of Net Monthly Income; etc...
