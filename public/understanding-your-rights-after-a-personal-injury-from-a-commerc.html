Understanding Your Rights After a

Personal Injury from a Commercial

Vehicle

If you have been injured in an accident involving a commercial vehicle, you need to understand your rights. Commercial vehicles are those used for business purposes, such as buses, trucks, or taxis. In most cases, the company responsible for operating the vehicle is liable for any damages that you may have suffered.

This article will discuss the legal process for recovering compensation after a personal injury caused by a commercial vehicle. It will also cover the elements of negligence law, the statute of limitations, the types of damages you can recover, and more!

Negligence Law and Determining Liability in Personal Injury Cases

Negligence law is an important part of any personal injury claim involving a commercial vehicle. The elements of negligence law include duty, breach of duty, causation, and damages. In short, the defendant must have owed a duty of care to the plaintiff under the law for there to be a negligence claim. The defendant must then have breached that duty of care, causing the plaintiff to suffer damages.

Establishing Fault in an Accident Involving a Commercial Vehicle

In order to prove fault in an accident involving a commercial vehicle, the plaintiff must show negligence on the part of the driver or company in question. This could include distracted driving, operating a vehicle while under the influence of drugs or alcohol, speeding, failing to maintain the vehicle or any other activity that could be considered negligent under the law.

Statute of Limitations and Filing Deadlines

The statute of limitations is a law that sets a time limit for filing certain types of legal claims, including personal injury cases. In most states, the statute of limitations is two years from the date of the accident. However, this can vary from state to state, and it is important to know your state's specific requirements.

How to Determine Your Filing Deadline

The best way to determine your filing deadline is to consult with a personal injury lawyer. They will be able to review your case and provide you with the best advice on how to proceed. They can also help you understand the statute of limitations in your particular state, as well as any special filing requirements you may need to meet.

The Different Types of Damages You May Be Able To Recover

You may be able to recover various types of damages, depending on the specifics of your case. These could include:

  • Medical expenses: These are the costs of any medical care you may have needed as a result of your injury. This could include hospital or doctor bills, medication costs, physical therapy, and more.
  • Lost wages: You may also be able to recover lost wages for any time you were unable to work because of your injury.
  • Pain and suffering: Pain and suffering is a type of non-economic damage that can be recovered in personal injury cases. This can include physical pain, mental anguish, emotional distress, and more.
  • Punitive damages: In some cases, punitive damages may also be available. This is a type of damage awarded as a form of punishment for particularly reckless or negligent behavior.

    • A personal injury lawyer can help you understand the types of compensation you may be eligible for, as well as any applicable laws or regulations. They can also help you navigate the legal process and guide you through every step of your case.

    How To Prove Liability in a Commercial Vehicle Accident

    In order to prove liability in a commercial vehicle accident, you must be able to demonstrate that the driver or company involved was negligent. This means showing that they owed a duty of care to you, the plaintiff, and that their actions breached this duty. You must then be able to show that this breach of duty caused you to suffer damages.

    To do this, you will need to collect evidence and build your case. This could include eyewitness testimony, photos, video or audio recordings, medical reports, and more. Your lawyer can help you understand what type of evidence is necessary for your case and how to go about collecting it.
