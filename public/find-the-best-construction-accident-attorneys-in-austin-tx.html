Common Causes of Construction Accidents in Austin

Construction workers are at risk of suffering severe construction accident injuries or death due to on-site accidents caused by the “Fatal Four”: falls, electrocutions, being struck by objects, and being trapped in/between hazards. The recently reported crane accident that wounded 20 people serves as a reminder of how rapidly an incident can occur and place those present in danger.

In order to protect themselves from similar predicaments, it is vital for personnel involved with construction sites to be aware of these most usual causes and what preventive measures must be taken when applicable. Through understanding risks associated with common pitfalls like “Fatal Four” mentioned above, one may strive toward securing their safety as much as possible while working on-site, premises.

Falls

Falls are the number one culprit in construction mishaps and often occur due to inadequate fall protection, unstable work surfaces, worker inexperience, and a lack of occupational safety protocols properly issued by the employer. Unsecured edges and openings, misuse of portable ladders, unsteady scaffolding units, incomplete roofs, and a lack of guardrails contribute greatly to occurrences of falls at building sites.

In order for safety on-site to be maintained, it is imperative that appropriate training as well as measures aimed at preventing occupational hazards such as fall prevention must be put into practice. This ensures employees recognize all potential dangers associated with working above ground levels while being equipped correctly with necessary protective gear against falling incidents.

Electrocutions

When it comes to the safety of construction workers, electrocutions can be a serious issue that may arise from problems with wiring, lack of grounding, or contact with power lines. If an accident occurs due to electrical shock, there are several issues such as burns, nerve damage, and organ dysfunction which could lead to severe injuries or even construction accident fatalities.

It is important for those affected by this type of incident on building sites to first seek medical attention before filing reports and looking into consultation options like reaching out to a construction accident attorney who specializes in these matters.

Struck by Objects

Construction site accidents can lead to serious injuries for construction workers if they are struck by falling objects like tools and materials. Injuries sustained from such incidents could range from simple cuts or bruises, broken bones, and head trauma up to fatality. It is important that injured construction workers consult with a lawyer specialized in this type of accident as well as seek medical help and report the incident immediately afterward.

A trustworthy professional offering representation for these kinds of occurrences would be able to assist survivors or their families receive just compensation after a traumatic experience resulting from being hit at the work site.

Caught-In/Between Hazards

Construction accidents that involve being caught in or between equipment, materials, and structures are often associated with severe injuries. Unguarded moving parts of machines, collapsing constructions as well as getting pinned between objects are the main culprits leading to this kind of hazard. The risks may result in serious trauma ranging from fractures to amputations and even death. It is important for workers working on sites to have safety practices that can help mitigate these issues before an accident occurs.

Types of Compensation Available for Construction Accident Victims

Victims of construction accidents can seek relief with a construction accident claim, providing coverage for medical bills and other financial losses such as lost wages and earning capacity. The level of compensation awarded will depend on the severity of injuries sustained from the incident in question.

In this article, we explore how various forms of compensation may help alleviate some costs incurred due to a construction-related injury.

Medical Expenses

Medical costs from a construction accident can involve hospital bills, medication charges, physical therapy, and other needed medical attention. By having a personal injury lawyer represent you in the case, compensation for these expenses- past and present- could be attained with sufficient proof. Such legal assistance may make sure that one receives all they are entitled to when it comes to damages related to their experience of an incident onsite at the building project site.

Lost Wages and Earning Capacity

The lost wages and earning capacity compensation received by the injured individual may take into consideration not only what they didn’t receive while recovering from an accident, but also any possible future earnings that have been affected due to their injury. This could mean income loss caused by lack of employment as well as potentially reducing the ability to earn in the near future because of said injury. Reimbursement for prospective salaries is part of these benefits - the victim can be compensated for losing out on paychecks if it’s a direct result of being hurt or harmed during an incident.

Pain and Suffering

When it comes to pain and suffering compensation, the severity of an injury resulting from a construction accident as well as any related medical costs must be taken into consideration. This form of financial reimbursement covers physical pain, emotional distress, mental anguish and other types of psychological or physical harm that impacts one’s quality of life due to this unfortunate occurrence. Losses such as loss of enjoyment are also factored when assessing how much should be awarded for damages suffered by the victim. This would also include construction fatalities and compensation to family members for their loss.
