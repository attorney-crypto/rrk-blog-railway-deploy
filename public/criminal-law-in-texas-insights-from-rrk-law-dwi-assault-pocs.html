Here is a general overview to give you an example of the complexities and charges for various POCS arrests.

In Texas, it is unlawful for any person knowingly or intentionally to possess a controlled substance unless such substance was obtained directly, or pursuant to a valid prescription or order, from a practitioner while acting in the course of his professional practice.

Possession of less than one gram of certain controlled substances is a state jail felony, while possession of one to four grams is a third degree felony. For other controlled substances like marijuana, possession of two ounces or less is a class B misdemeanor, while more than four ounces but less than five pounds is a state jail felony.

A person convicted of possession can face penalties ranging from a class B misdemeanor up to a first or second degree felony depending on the amount and type of substance. For example, possession of 50-2000 pounds of marijuana is a second degree felony
