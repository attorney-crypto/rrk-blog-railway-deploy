What is an SR-22?

An SR-22 is a type of certificate or form that is filed by an insurance company to the Department of Public Safety (DPS) on behalf of a driver in order to prove that they have liability insurance.

This is typically used for high-risk drivers who are required to maintain coverage in order to keep their licenses valid. The SR-22 serves as proof that the driver has purchased liability insurance and will remain covered for the specified period of time. An SR-22 is often required for a minimum coverage policy.

In most cases, the SR-22 must be filed for three consecutive years in order for the driver’s license to remain valid.

The SR-22 is often referred to as a “certificate of financial responsibility” and provides evidence that a driver has the necessary level of auto liability insurance coverage.

What is an ignition interlock device?

An ignition interlock device (IID) is a breathalyzer-type machine that prevents a vehicle from starting if the driver has been drinking.

DWI Texas requires all drivers convicted of driving while intoxicated (DWI) to install an IID in their vehicles for a period of time.

An IID works by having the driver blow into the device before starting the car. If the breath sample registers a blood alcohol content (BAC) above the pre-set limit – usually 0.02% – then the vehicle won’t start.

In addition, random rolling retests require that drivers submit to breathalyzer tests while driving, and if they fail they will be unable to continue operating their vehicle until they pass another test.

Car insurance companies view DWI/DUI convictions in much the same way as any other reckless behavior behind the wheel, so depending on your company’s policy you could face significantly higher premiums or even revocation of coverage altogether if you are convicted of drunk driving in Texas.

The installation of an IID could potentially help lower your premiums after being convicted since it shows that you are taking steps to prevent such behavior from recurring in the future.

However, many car insurance providers may still see your conviction as an indicator of heightened risk and charge a higher rate regardless of any mitigating factors such as installing an IID.
