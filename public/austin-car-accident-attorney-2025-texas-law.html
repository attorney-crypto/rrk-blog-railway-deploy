2025–2026 TEXAS LAW

Texas Transportation Code § 550.021 requires any driver involved in an accident resulting in injury, death, or property damage to remain at the scene. Texas follows a modified comparative fault rule under Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 33.001 — a plaintiff may recover damages so long as their fault does not exceed 50%. The statute of limitations for personal injury claims in Texas is two years from the date of injury under Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 16.003. Austin car accident claims are filed in Travis County District Court. Recoverable damages include medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering, mental anguish, and property damage. What should I do immediately after a car accident in Austin?

Call 911, remain at the scene, document the other driver's insurance and license information, photograph all vehicles and road conditions, collect witness contact information, and seek medical treatment the same day. Do not give a recorded statement to any insurance company before speaking with an Austin car accident attorney.

How long do I have to file a car accident lawsuit in Texas?

Two years from the date of the accident under Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 16.003. Missing this deadline permanently bars your claim regardless of fault or injury severity.

What is my Austin car accident case worth?

Value depends on medical expenses, lost income, permanency of injury, liability clarity, and available insurance coverage. Travis County car accident cases involving serious injury regularly resolve in the six-figure range. Soft tissue cases with full recovery settle for significantly less. No attorney can ethically guarantee a specific outcome.

What if the other driver was uninsured in Texas?

Texas requires uninsured/underinsured motorist coverage to be offered to all policyholders under Tex. Ins. Code § 1952.101. If you rejected it in writing, you may have limited recovery options against an uninsured driver. If you have UM/UIM coverage, your own insurer steps in to cover the gap.

Can I still recover if I was partially at fault for the accident?

Yes, as long as your fault does not exceed 50% under Texas modified comparative fault. Your recovery is reduced proportionally by your percentage of fault. If you are found 30% at fault on a $100,000 claim, you recover $70,000.

What is the punitive damages cap in Texas?

Exemplary damages under Tex. Civ. Prac. & Rem. Code § 41.008 are capped at the greater of: (a) $200,000, or (b) two times economic damages plus noneconomic damages not to exceed $750,000. This cap was not amended by the 89th Texas Legislature. It applies to general civil litigation; medical malpractice has separate caps under Chapter 74.
