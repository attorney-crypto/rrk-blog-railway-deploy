What Compensation Can I Get if I'm Injured in an Austin Car Accident?

In Texas, those who have been affected by car accidents are usually eligible for compensation that corresponds to the magnitude of their losses. Therefore, there is not a single average settlement amount per accident - every case must be analyzed individually.

Texas car accident victims may be able to obtain compensation for both economic and non-economic damages. Examples of commonly sought compensatory awards include:

Medical Expenses

You are entitled to reimbursement for both your prior and upcoming medical expenses. These payments could cover the entirety of your treatment costs, including:

Doctor visits

Hospitalization

Surgery

Physical therapy

Prescription drugs

Diagnostic tests

Counseling and mental health treatment

Assistive aids such as a wheelchair

Lost Wages

After the accident, your sustained injuries may impair your capacity to earn income. An experienced attorney can help you demonstrate that these injuries have hindered your ability to make a living and acquire appropriate monetary compensation for it.

Property Damage

In the unfortunate event of an accident, vehicles are often left with lasting damage - or even totaled altogether. Regrettably, such incidents can significantly lower a vehicle's value in spite of any repairs that may be made.

Documenting the scene of a collision is essential after an accident if it's possible. Snap pictures to document any damage done to vehicles and don't forget to keep records for every expense incurred due to the crash - such as:

Towing costs

Bodywork

Cost of rental car

Vehicle repairs

Parking fees

Mileage reimbursement for travel to doctor’s appointments

Scarring and Disfigurement

Car accident victims often seek reparation for the disfigurements caused by the incident, such as amputations and scarring. Not all claims require a pronounced blemish; embarrassment suffered by the victim is taken into consideration with each claim, even if they choose to conceal their injury from public view.

Loss of Enjoyment of Life

In these claims, the victim's physical impairments are based on their reduced capacity to appreciate activities after they've had a car crash. For example, this could mean not being able to participate in leisure events or not being able to enjoy certain foods due to facial injuries.

Pain and suffering

Crash victims deserve to be compensated for the emotional and physical trauma they experienced as a direct result of their injuries. Victims may seek financial remuneration to cover past, present, and future anguish caused by this tragedy.

Mental Anguish

Mental anguish is a deep sense of mental torment that surpasses feelings such as embarrassment, fear, and anger. Your lawyer will work diligently to procure the right evidence in order to demonstrate that you have experienced this level of severe distress due to the accident.

Punitive Damages

Punitive damages are awarded under special circumstances to punish drivers who behave recklessly and with extreme negligence. These financial awards serve as a warning that such actions will not be condoned by the court.

MAIN FACTORS IN TEXAS STATE CAR CRASHES

Driving under the influence

Texas has long been plagued by alcohol-related tragedies, with drunk driving accidents remaining one of the leading causes of serious crashes.

The Foundation for Advancing Alcohol Responsibility reported that in 2014 alone, 1,446 lives were lost due to these accidents – equating to over 40% of all traffic fatalities across the state; a much higher rate than the national average (30.3%) for that same year.

In 2015, Texas passed a law initiated by Mothers Against Drunk Driving which mandated that all DUI offenders must have an ignition interlock device installed in their vehicles.

This legislation has the potential to reduce re-offending and associated collisions drastically. Despite its effectiveness, it does not tackle the issue of first-time offenders or those who frequently drink and drive but evade apprehension from authorities.

Illegal or unsafe travel speeds

Unsafe or excessive speed is a major factor when it comes to serious accidents in Texas. According to the TDOT, over 17,000 crashes that happened during 2015 were due to drivers driving too fast or not following the speed limit – resulting in 398 lives lost and more than 3,300 people injured in serious car accidents.

Furthermore, those who didn't adjust their speeds properly while trying to avoid collisions were involved in another 30,000+ incidents which caused an additional 160 deaths and more than 3 thousand documented injuries.

Although it is not the most common cause of accidents in Texas, speeding may contribute to serious injuries and deaths disproportionately.

According to The Insurance Institute for Highway Safety, drivers have less time to respond appropriately when they are traveling at excessively high speeds as well as reduced control over their vehicles.

In addition, these types of crashes release an expanded amount of energy that heightens risks such as structural damage inflicted on cars or more severe results like injury to passengers.

Driver inattention or distraction

In 2015, the Texas Department of Transportation reported that distracted driving was attributed to over 105,000 car accidents throughout the state.

Of these unfortunate events, 2,500 resulted in incapacitating injuries, and 10,000 more caused less severe wounds; even more tragically, 422 lives were lost in motor vehicle accidents. It is essential for everyone on the roadways to be aware of their surroundings at all times to ensure the safety of all drivers and passengers.

According to KXAN News, driver distraction has become a factor in one out of every five accidents in Texas. Although multiple efforts have been made over the years to enact a state-wide ban on cell phone use while driving - which is an enormous source of distraction - none have proven successful.

A few cities have implemented local bans instead, yet this apparently has not had much impact on reducing the rate of distracted driving incidents across the entire state.
