Qualifications for Expunction in Austin, Texas

Understanding the qualifications and criteria for expunction in Austin, Texas is crucial. Expunction, also known as expungement, is the legal process of erasing or sealing criminal records, providing individuals with a fresh start. Here are key factors to consider for determining if your case qualifies for expunction: Eligibility criteria for expunction in Austin, Texas: To be eligible for expunction, certain criteria must be met. In Austin, Texas, these generally include:

  • Charges were dismissed or you were acquitted at trial
  • You were pardoned for the offense
  • The case was not filed within the statute of limitations
  • Certain Class C misdemeanor deferred dispositions were completed
  • You were arrested but never formally charged

    • Important Note: Completing a sentence or probation does NOT typically qualify for expunction. If you successfully completed deferred adjudication probation, you may qualify for an Order of Nondisclosure instead, which seals (rather than destroys) your record. Expunction is generally reserved for cases that ended in dismissal, acquittal, or were never formally charged.

    Expunction eligibility depends on case OUTCOME, not offense type: Under Texas Code of Criminal Procedure Chapter 55, expunction may be available when:

  • Charges were dismissed
  • You were acquitted at trial
  • You received a pardon

    • Determining if your case qualifies for expunction: Consulting an experienced expunction lawyer is crucial to determine if your case meets the qualifications for expunction. They will meticulously evaluate the details of your case, such as the nature of the offense, previous convictions, and other relevant factors.     Petition for Nondisclosure: An Alternative Option

    If you're not eligible for expunction, you may still have options to limit access to your criminal record through a petition for nondisclosure. In Texas, nondisclosure is a legal process that seals certain criminal records from public view, although they remain visible to law enforcement and some government agencies. Eligibility for Petition for Nondisclosure: To be eligible for a petition for nondisclosure in Texas, you must meet certain criteria:

  • Successfully completed deferred adjudication community supervision
  • Waited the required time period after completing your sentence
  • Not been convicted of certain offenses that are ineligible for nondisclosure

    • The non disclosure form Texas is a crucial document in this process. It must be filled out accurately and completely to initiate the nondisclosure procedure. Benefits of Non Disclosure in Texas: Obtaining a non disclosure in Texas can have significant benefits:

  • Improved employment prospects
  • Better housing opportunities
  • Increased educational options

    • The process for nondisclosure Texas is similar to expunction but with different eligibility requirements and outcomes. While expunction completely removes the record, nondisclosure seals it from public view.
