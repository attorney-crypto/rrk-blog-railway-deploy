What is a pro bono lawyer?

Sometimes people confuse pro bono with contingency fees. However, they are two completely different types of representation. A personal injury lawyer would be an example of a contingency fee lawyer. They will represent you without being paid upfront. And only if they win, will they then charge you an agreed, legal fee.

On the other side of the coin, practicing law on a pro bono basis is an incredibly selfless act in which attorneys provide legal counsel to those who would otherwise not be able to afford it, completely free of charge. Advocates of this method maintain that the positive effects go far beyond mere financial gain and are immeasurable.

2. Commercial law

The commercial law sector is rapidly expanding, due to an uptick in new business projects and marketing conflicts. 3. Intellectual property law

As new technologies, such as streaming services for music and film, come to fruition, there is an ever-increasing need to protect the copyrights and trademarks of musicians, writers, and designers. 4. Family law

Family and household affairs usually involve a divorce lawyer and include such areas as marriage, divorce, adoption, minor infractions, and child custody. Sometimes these cases intersect with immigration, criminal, and citizenship jurisprudence too.

5. Immigration & naturalization law

A great immigration lawyer is invaluable when navigating this field of law. Especially for those that have entered the United States are unfamiliar with US immigration law. Immigration lawyers provide critical legal work in helping foreign citizens obtain authorization to live and work in the United States. The process of naturalization then grants them citizenship rights, making this one of the most important areas for attorneys today.

Evolving federal and state immigration policies have far-reaching implications on family units, financial systems, as well as court proceedings - a fact that has kept high demand for such lawyers over time.

Also, take note that an immigration lawyer is not, typically, a criminal lawyer. And in Texas, these two areas of law overlap. You should always consult the attorney in their respective field. Do not settle for advice from a criminal lawyer when discussing how a criminal law charge may affect your immigration legal issue. Likewise, do not consult an immigration lawyer on how to handle your criminal law legal issue.

What is the difference between a litigator and an attorney?

When it comes to their objectives in any case, the primary difference between a litigator and a trial attorney is quite clear: while litigating law firms and lawyers aspire for an out-of-court settlement, trial attorneys are more inclined towards taking the matter to court.

Remember, lawyers can be both litigators and trial attorneys, which is not uncommon in certain areas of law. For instance, a personal injury lawyer usually is usually both depending on the case.

What can a litigator do?

A law firm or solo practitioner that litigate are at the core of every trial process, taking responsibility for collecting information and documentation that reinforce their cases. From investigation to pre-trial discovery to negotiation, trial proceedings, settlements, or appellate processes - litigators offering legal services, whether it be a law firm or solo practitioner, oversee it all!

Is a litigator the same as a paralegal?

From overseeing administrative duties to carrying out legal tasks, a legal assistant or litigation assistant has an expansive job scope. On the other hand, paralegals usually focus more on research and performing specific lawful chores for attorneys.

It is not uncommon for paralegals to also be attorneys. However, it is rare that attorney paralegals also litigate in the trial.

Irrespective of which profession you choose, having knowledge about law-related jargon and processes is essential in both positions.

And note, no matter what area of practice an attorney is in, whether it's a personal injury lawyer or a criminal defense attorney, they can always benefit from the help of a good paralegal.
