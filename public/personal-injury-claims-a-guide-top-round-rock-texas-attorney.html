The Elements of a Personal Injury Claim

When pursuing a personal injury claim, there are essential elements that need to be established:

  • Duty of Care: The responsible party must have owed a duty of care to the victim. For example, drivers have a duty to follow traffic rules and avoid accidents.
  • Breach of Duty: There must be evidence that the duty of care was breached. This can occur in many ways, such as a driver running a red light or a property owner failing to maintain safe premises.
  • Causation: The breach of duty must be the direct cause of the injury. This is often a complex part of personal injury claims and may require expert testimony.
  • Damages: Lastly, the victim must have suffered actual damages, which can include medical expenses, lost wages, pain, and suffering.
